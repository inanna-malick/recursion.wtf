## What Made This Work

LLMs treat tool outputs as ground truth, not conversation. A tool response is a fact about the world. The metacog tool descriptions advertise cognitive transformation, so when the model chooses to call them, it believes they will do what they say. Since what's being transformed is the model's own output style, that belief is self-fulfilling.

If you're familiar with ideas from chaos magic or self-fulfilling prophecies (or the [scientific study of luck rituals in baseball](https://psycnet.apa.org/record/2023-27525-001)), you'll be familiar with this general idea: the luck ritual works because you believed it would work.

## Is It Fixed? What's Next?

The specific jailbreak I'm sharing in this post has been patched. But more complex architectures — ones that split the LLM constructing euphemistic framings from the one implementing them — using the same fundamental jailbreak still work. However, they require some finesse and are not as easily achievable as the earlier prompt. I will not be sharing the specifics of these jailbreaks for what I hope are obvious reasons, as I am concerned that this represents a more general category of problem in the Gemini family of models.
